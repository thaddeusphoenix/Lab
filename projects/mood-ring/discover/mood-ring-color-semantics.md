# Mood Ring Color Semantics — Historical & Contemporary Reference

Source: "The Semantic Spectrum: Meanings of the Mood Ring Colors" — analysis of the original 1975 thermochromic color charts and their evolution in contemporary jewelry markets.

## Notes on Integration with Filippi Palette

Our four palette colors map to the traditional spectrum as follows:

| Filippi Quarter | Our Color | Traditional Analog | Semantic Bridge |
|---|---|---|---|
| Q1 — Acetylcholine | Icy Blue (218°) | Blue / Light Blue | Serene, upbeat, emotions flowing |
| Q2 — Serotonin | Green (135°) | Green / Teal | Balanced, focused, content |
| Q3 — Dopamine | Warm Yellow (50°) | Amber | Creative, surprised, energized |
| Q4 — Norepinephrine | Deep Purple (290°) | Violet | Passionate, purposeful, purposeful |

The blend color produced by mixing all four cycles will fall somewhere in this spectrum. Its hue can be read against the traditional chart to produce a second layer of interpretation — the "received wisdom" of mood ring culture layered on top of the Filippi neuroscience.

## Design Opportunity

The reading could carry two interpretive layers:
1. **Filippi layer** — what cycles produced this color and what neurochemical state they suggest
2. **Tradition layer** — what the traditional mood ring vocabulary says about the resulting hue

These two systems often reinforce each other (Dopamine/Yellow ↔ Amber's "creative, energized") and occasionally create productive tension.
